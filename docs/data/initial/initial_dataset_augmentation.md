# Context & Objective
This document defines algorithm specs for synthetic data augmentation for initial `purchases` and `users_attribution` tables.
See also: __./initial_data_discoveries.md__ for the findings leading to the necessity of this process.

# 1. `purchases` table augmentation
1 Issue: revenue data is sporadic (@see __./initial_data_discoveries.md__ 1.1), with no clear cycles / patterns to explain per-month shocks in revenue acquisition. Given that the observed period is short (11 months), it's impossible to capture meaningful seasonality / holiday effects for ANY time-series models. We are left with 2 choices: artificially restructure the ledger log to provide these cycles on a WEEKLY basis (lowest frequency of the data), or embed the knowledge about the specific business model experiment's subject operates in into all MMM models (through exog. vars like seasonality PLUS Bayesian priors).
**Decision — Chosen approach: Embed domain knowledge as exogenous variables INTO the models (Approach B).**

**Concrete steps:**
Global MMM:
    - Add Fourier seasonality terms (sin/cos pair for quarterly cycle) to `mmm_timeseries`.
    - Add structural variable for observable regime changes: `revenue_anomaly_score` - per-region 8-week rolling z-score of net revenue. It is region-specific and data-driven - a better replacement for any collinear global dummies.
    - `is_sep_nov_trough` and `is_structural_peak` are binary flags derived from the z-score.
Bayesian MMM:
    - Set Bayesian priors that encode the expected direction of these effects.
    - For BSTS: rely on the 2-order LLT to capture the macro drift organically.


2 Issue: in the 11-month `purchases`, the initial volume of transactions is ~244k entries. Total revenue, after subtracting refunds, is ~2,440kk USD. While in the `insights` table, the total spend is ~4,269kk USD - this gives us a NEGATIVE (57%) ROI. This is already a bad sign, but it gets worse. 29% of this ad spends are generated for 5 biggest country segments located in different regions: US, MX, BR, RU, UA. Their cummulative revenue is ~565k USD - this gives us $\approx$ 13.2% of total revenue is generated from these countries - giving us even worse picture of ROAS for them (13.2% / 29% $\approx$ 0.46). The CAC is also bad: ex. US has 3k distinct attributions, total spend of 387k USD, CAC of 129$, and ARPU of 36,6$. This is just absurd. ideally, LTV > 2.5x CAC.
---
These observations, alongside with very low global retention (53,4% for first rebill, 30% for second; even though edu-apps often see MRR around 67%), suggest that the initial ledger is heavily skewed downwards and typically would require significant augmentation (towards PROFITABILITY and better KPI scores) to produce business-actionable results. This is not the focus of this thesis, however.
**The correct response is to NOT augment the purchases table.** This is not a concession - it IS the argument:
- The business's raw data is noisy, sparse, and shows a negative ROI ($1.37M net revenue vs. $4.27M ad spend ≈ -68% ROI). The business's current deterministic strategy (Last-Click + OLS + exponential decay) cannot produce actionable insights from this data precisely because heuristic methods are not robust to noise.
- The probabilistic pipeline's comparative advantage is demonstrated by its ability to extract calibrated, uncertainty-aware forecasts FROM this noisy reality, not from a cleaned-up version of it. A probabilistic model that honestly reports "wide credible intervals" on poor-KPI data is strictly more useful than a deterministic model that confidently commits to a wrong point estimate.
- The thesis statement ("probabilistic >> deterministic") is strengthened, not weakened, by operating on the raw data. If both approaches produce poor absolute numbers, the probabilistic approach wins by producing *calibrated confidence bounds* around those poor numbers — enabling the business to make risk-aware budget decisions rather than acting on false precision.


# 2. `users_attribution` table augmentation
Issue: per-country data sparsity in the raw table is significant (51 countries with <= 15 attributions; 27 regions mapped to macro-groups). Naively copying rows would amplify existing last-click bias into the DDA. The goal is to raise the observed traffic volume for qualifying cells up to a minimum density threshold **without fabricating new channel-preference signals** by imputing currently missing granular data.
Note: every sub/customer generated by #1 above **MUST have an attribution** to a paid / organic channel.
**Important Note:** not every advertisement campaing should be profitable, and not every country MUST have attributions at all (for ex., some countries might have 0 organic traffic, and some might have 0 paid traffic).
---
**Decision: conservative density-floor imputation (region-level redistribution only).**
Since purchases are NOT being augmented (no new synthetic customers), this section only applies to addressing the per-country sparsity of the EXISTING users' attribution records. The goal is to ensure that the DDA models have sufficient per-region traffic volume for stable Markov transition matrices and Shapley coalitions.

**Algorithm — Region-Proportional Redistribution:**
1. **Eligibility:** A `(country_code, media_source)` pair is eligible for imputation only if it has **at least 1** observed attribution record in the raw data.
2. **Density floor:** For each `macro_region`, compute the median attribution count per country within that region. Countries below 50% of this median are candidates for upward imputation.
3. **Imputation method:** For each candidate country, sample additional attribution records from the regional pool (same `macro_region`, same `media_source`) using the empirical `created_at` distribution of the candidate country. This preserves temporal patterns without fabricating new channel-preference signals.
4. **Volume cap:** The total imputed attributions per country must not exceed the regional median. This prevents any single sparse country from receiving disproportionate synthetic traffic.
5. **ROCV compliance:** Imputation must use only records within the current fold's training window. Holdout-period attributions are never used as imputation sources.
6. **Convergence with purchases:** Since no new purchases are being fabricated, the imputed attribution records will have `is_conversion = False` in `touchpoints_log` (they represent website visits / ad impressions that did NOT convert). This adds churn paths to the Markov chain without inflating conversion counts.
**Note:** this imputation is modest by design - it raises the statistical floor for sparse countries without materially changing the global channel weight distribution. The previous approach failed because it generated conversion-bearing attributions tied to fabricated purchases; this approach generates non-converting traffic only.


# 3. `insights` table augmentation
The raw table has significant data sparsity (in terms of amount of spend; 20 countries has <100$, 69 have <=1000$; 82 with avg spend <=5$, 29 with <=1$), which is still enough to produce stable statistical mass (334 days x 242 countries = 80,828 potential records, 66k is present, giving $\approx$ 81% density). This table probably SHOULD NOT be transformed, but in must converge with augmentations from #1 and #2. So actual (day, country) empirical distributions should be kept in mind, given that ad spendings typically correlate with the revenue data (not exactly proportionally or immediately (recall AdStock law)).
**Decision — Do NOT augment the insights table.** The 81% density is sufficient for all downstream models - no further augmentation is required.
